1. Qualifications

Operators must be fully trained to handle all transit assignments and cannot refuse based on route unfamiliarity.

Plain English: If you’re a Spareboard driver, you must be trained to drive any route. Maps or guides are enough — you can’t refuse a route because you don’t know it.

2. Safety Code

Rules include driving limits: 13 hours max driving/day, 14 hours duty/day, 16 hours total time/day, 8 hours rest required, and max 70 hours per 7 days.

Plain English: You have legal time limits on work. Don’t go over or skip breaks — it’s unsafe and illegal.

3. General Rules

Rules on removing names, signing sheets, work conflicts, overtime eligibility, and booking off.

Plain English: You can’t erase your name or sign for others. Mistakes can cost you seniority or pay. Follow sign-up rules strictly.
